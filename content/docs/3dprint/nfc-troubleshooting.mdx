# NFC Troubleshooting

Most issues with NFC keychains are related to phone positioning or environmental interference. Follow these steps to resolve common problems.

## Common Issues

### The tag isn't being detected
*   **Locate the Antenna:** Most phones have the NFC antenna near the camera module. You may need to slide your phone slowly over the keychain to find the exact "sweet spot."
*   **Remove Interference:** Ensure your keychain is not resting on a metal surface or inside a metal desk, as metal blocks NFC signals.
*   **Phone Case Thickness:** If you have a thick, rugged, or battery-extended case, try removing it to see if the signal improves.

### "Write failed" or "Tag busy" errors
*   **Steady Pressure:** If the write process fails, you likely moved the keychain during the transfer. Keep the phone and keychain held firmly against each other until the app says "Write Successful."
*   **Erase First:** If the tag already contains data, it is sometimes cleaner to perform an **"Erase tag"** command before attempting to write a new, large record.

## Important Warnings

<Callout type="warn" title="Do NOT Lock Your Tag">
  In the **NFC Tools** app, you may see an option to "Lock" the tag. 
  **Do not do this.** 
  Once a tag is locked, it becomes read-only forever and can never be updated or erased again. There is no way to undo this.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info" title="Tag Storage Capacity">
  These NFC tags have a limited memory capacity (typically 500 bytes). If you are trying to write a very long URL, the app will warn you if it exceeds the limit. Use a [URL shortener](https://bitly.com) if your link is too long.
</Callout>
